Instructions for use

Instructions for Use:

Caution – US Federal law restricts this device to sale by or on the order of a licensed Dentist.

Indications for Use:

Indicated for orthodontic movement of natural teeth. The Ortho-EZ System is a virtual manufacturing orthodontic appliance system used to correct malocclusions in orthodontic patients using patient specific orthodontic appliances.

There are some clinical scenarios that fall outside the scope of the Ortho-EZ system Final determination on case capabilities will be at the discretion of the treating clinician.

Ortho-EZ consists of patient-specific 3d printed plastic brackets, made in-office by the treating clinician. When directly bonding brackets, follow standard orthodontic office procedures. Ortho-EZ does not provide commercially available arch wires, ligatures, or adhesives. All use of such products should be used according to the manufacturer’s instructions.

Contraindications:

Ortho-EX brackets should not be bonded to dental porcelain, restorative or compromised enamel surfaces.

Warnings:

Ortho-Ez Brackets are for single patient use only. Due to the hardness of plastic brackets, bonding brackets in occlusion should be avoided to prevent wearing of enamel surfaces during all phases of treatment. Bonding of brackets to compromised teeth (i.e., with large restorations, peg laterals, or preexisting pathological conditions) can increase the risk of tooth damage.

Precautions:

Ortho-EZ Brackets are capable of withstanding all normal torque requirements. However, care should be taken when making torqueing activations. Large corrections with full size stainless steel wires may result in bracket failure and should be avoided. Instruct not to chew or bite hard substances such as hard candy, ice, or carrots. Careful and thorough patient instruction is a key to avoiding appliance or enamel damage. Bonding to porcelain crowns or facings may cause chipping or breakage of the crown or facing during treatment or debonding. Debonding of Ortho-EZ Brackets can be done using a direct bond bracket remover or similar instrument.

Adverse Reactions:

Plastic allergies.
